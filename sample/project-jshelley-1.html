"A Sonnet to Typos" by: Jonathan Shelley

A sonnet to typos Lying hidden among your brethren true, You strike at the moments least convenient. Turning the learned man a foolish hue, Betraying all his best and plain intent. I sometimes think of all that you destroy. The books, the claims, and all the dreams within Come crashing down because of your deploy, As if your show is tantamount to sin. That so small an error could feel so big, Is testament to your steady power. But in the holes for us you always dig, I give you repute that is far less sour. To err is human, to forgive, divine, You keep us human in the written line.
